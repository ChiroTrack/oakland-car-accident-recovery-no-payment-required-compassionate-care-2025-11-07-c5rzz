Privacy Policy

Your Privacy and Data Protection Matter to Us

At The Shift Chiropractic, we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have a positive experience on our website and in our practice.

Introduction

This Privacy Policy ("Policy") explains how The Shift Chiropractic ("we," "us," "our," or "Company") collects, uses, discloses, and safeguards your information when you visit our website and utilize our services. Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our website or services.

We reserve the right to make changes to this Privacy Policy at any time and for any reason. We will alert you about any changes by updating the "Last Updated" date of this Privacy Policy. Any changes or modifications will be effective immediately upon posting the updated Privacy Policy on the website, and you waive the right to receive specific notice of each such change or modification.

Your continued use of the website following the posting of revised Privacy Policy means that you accept and agree to the changes. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy frequently to stay informed of how we are protecting your information.

Information We Collect

Personal Information You Provide

We collect information that you voluntarily provide to us when you register on our website, request services, or communicate with us. This may include:

  • Name, email address, phone number, and mailing address
  • Medical history, health conditions, and insurance information
  • Payment information and billing details
  • Appointment preferences and treatment history
  • Any other information you choose to provide

Automatically Collected Information

When you access our website, we automatically collect certain information about your device and browsing behavior, including:

  • IP address and browser type
  • Pages visited and time spent on our website
  • Referring website and search terms used
  • Device information and operating system
  • Location data (with your permission)

Information from Third Parties

We may receive information about you from third parties, including insurance companies, healthcare providers, personal injury attorneys, and other medical professionals involved in your care. This information helps us coordinate your treatment and ensure continuity of care.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for various purposes, including:

  • Medical Treatment and Care

    To provide, maintain, and improve our chiropractic and medical services, including diagnosing conditions, creating treatment plans, and monitoring your progress.

  • Billing and Payment Processing

    To process your payments, manage insurance claims, coordinate liens, and handle billing inquiries related to your medical services.

  • Communications

    To send appointment reminders, treatment updates, educational materials, and respond to your inquiries and requests.

  • Website Improvement

    To analyze website usage, understand user preferences, and improve our online services and user experience.

  • Legal Compliance

    To comply with legal obligations, respond to lawful requests from authorities, and protect our legal rights and the rights of our patients.

  • Security and Fraud Prevention

    To detect, prevent, and address fraud, security issues, and other potentially harmful or illegal activity.

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience on our website and collect information about how you interact with our site. Cookies are small data files stored on your device that help us recognize you and remember your preferences.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Required for website functionality, security, and service delivery.
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our website and improve our services.
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your choices and preferences for a personalized experience.
  • Marketing Cookies: Track your activity to deliver targeted advertising and promotional content.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or alert you when cookies are being sent. However, blocking cookies may affect the functionality of our website. For more information about cookies and how to manage them, visit www.allaboutcookies.org.

We also use Google Analytics and other third-party analytics services to track website performance and user behavior. These services may set their own cookies on your device. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of these third-party services.

Information Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your information in the following circumstances:

  • Healthcare Providers: We share medical information with other healthcare providers, specialists, and facilities involved in your care with your consent.
  • Insurance Companies: We share necessary information to process insurance claims and coordinate benefits.
  • Legal Counsel: We share information with your personal injury attorney or our legal counsel as needed for case management and legal proceedings.
  • Service Providers: We share information with vendors and service providers who assist us in operating our website and providing services.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information when required by law, court order, or government authority.
  • Business Transfers: Information may be transferred in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets.

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes. Any information sharing is done in accordance with HIPAA regulations and applicable privacy laws.

Data Security and Protection

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your personal and medical information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Our security measures include:

  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest using industry-standard protocols
  • Secure firewalls and intrusion detection systems
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Access controls and authentication procedures
  • Employee training on data protection and privacy practices
  • HIPAA compliance and adherence to healthcare privacy standards

While we strive to protect your information, no security system is completely impenetrable. We cannot guarantee absolute security. If a data breach occurs, we will notify affected individuals in accordance with applicable laws and regulations.

Your Rights and Choices

You have certain rights regarding your personal and medical information:

  • Access: You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction: You can request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Deletion: You may request deletion of your information, subject to legal and medical record retention requirements.
  • Opt-Out: You can opt out of marketing communications and certain data collection practices.
  • Portability: You have the right to request a copy of your information in a portable format.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided in the Contact Information section below. We will respond to your request within the timeframe required by applicable law.

Third-Party Links and Services

Our website may contain links to third-party websites and services that are not operated by us. This Privacy Policy does not apply to third-party websites, and we are not responsible for their privacy practices. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites before providing your information.

When you click on a third-party link, you leave our website and enter a different website. We have no control over the content, policies, or practices of these external sites.

Children's Privacy

Our website and services are not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that we have collected information from a child under 13, we will take steps to delete such information and terminate the child's account.

For patients under 18, we require parental or guardian consent and may collect information on their behalf. Parents and guardians may contact us to review or request deletion of their child's information.

HIPAA Compliance

As a healthcare provider, we comply with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and its Privacy Rule. Your medical information is protected under HIPAA regulations, and we maintain strict confidentiality standards.

We have implemented comprehensive administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to ensure the privacy and security of your Protected Health Information (PHI). Our HIPAA Privacy Notice provides additional details about how we use and protect your medical information.

If you have concerns about our HIPAA compliance or believe your privacy rights have been violated, you can file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services Office for Civil Rights.
